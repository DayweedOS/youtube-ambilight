YouTube Ambilight settings

Changes to this setting will take 4-5 seconds to show up.
Represents the amount of ambilight instances.
Increasing this setting strengthes the other settings except the Blur/Fade settings.
This setting might hurt performance when raised above 40!
Represents the spreading of multiple ambilight instances.
Increasing the Strength/Opacity setting strengthes the effect, but to much can result in a lot of distraction while watching the video and might be unrealistic.
This setting might hurt performance when raised above 50!
Represents the mixing of details in the video.
Increasing this setting gives the ambilight a more suddle but wide effect, but the color reproduction might suffer since details will be mixed more with each other.
Represents the dark/light of the video. (A minimum of 115 is recommended)
Increasing this setting gives more contrast, but to much contrast can also result in a lot of white or black spots. Lowering this setting to much results in a weak grey ambilight.
Represents the colorfullness of the video.
Increasing this setting gives more color, but to much saturation can also result in wrong red, green or blue colors. Lowering this setting to 0 results in a black and white ambilight.
Represents the whiteness of the video.
If the ambilight is to weak it's recommended to increase the Strenght/Opacity setting first.
Increasing this setting results in a brighter light, but to much can result in a white/grey ambilight.
